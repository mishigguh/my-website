Approach

Bodywork as hands-on education.

This work weaves together the principles of physical therapy, massage, and movement practice. It is hands-on body education: manual therapy that helps you relax, sense yourself more clearly, and create small changes that compound over time.

Most pain and tension is a pattern — not just a symptom. The same holding, the same compensation, the same area flaring up. The goal is not only to relax you and send you home. It is to help you understand what your body is doing and give it a new option.

It is a collaborative process. The more we can understand what you are working with, the more useful our time together becomes.

The Work

A session draws from whatever the body needs.

No session looks exactly the same. The approach is chosen based on what's happening and what's most useful — not a fixed protocol.

Deep Tissue & Myofascial Release Pressure and glides that shear stuck layers of fascia. Long holds that open the tissue at depth.
Sustained Pressure & Contact Holds, glides, traction, and carefully applied pressure to help areas that feel stuck, dense, or under-responsive.
Nervous System & Breath Breathing cues and attentive contact that support a more settled, open, and relaxed state.
Movement & Awareness Guided attention to how you hold, brace, and compensate. Small movements that give the body new information.
Table & Mat Work Sessions can happen on a massage table or a mat on the floor, depending on what serves the work best.
Injury History & Restricted Movement Careful work with people navigating old injuries, surgery history, scarring, or restricted movement when bodywork is appropriate.
What to Expect

A session is a conversation, not a protocol.

When you arrive

We start by talking.

We'll discuss the kind of work best suited for you — on the table or the mat — and what you're wanting to address. I'll listen and offer suggestions, and we'll collaboratively craft an approach to start with.

During the session

We unfold it together.

A session might be long holds in deep fascial stretches, sustained pressure and glides, traction, breath cues, or guided awareness that brings about a more relaxed, whole state. It follows the body's signals, not a script.

When you leave

Relief, relaxation, and new information.

Most people leave with a sense of ease they didn't walk in with — and some new understanding of what their body was holding. That clarity is part of what makes the work last.

What shifts

We can tell it's working when daily life has more ease and more options.

01

Less pain and tension

Chronic pain, tension, and discomfort decrease, or move into new patterns that can be addressed further.

02

More freedom to move

Moving without fear of pain — or without pain itself — becomes more available. Activities that were limited open back up.

03

More ease in daily life

End-of-day tension reduces. Sleep improves. Stress has somewhere to go. The body stops being something you're managing and becomes something you inhabit.

FAQ

A few common questions.

Is this massage?

It includes massage and manual therapy, but it is not only massage. The work is oriented around hands-on body education — relaxation, awareness, and changing tension patterns. If you want a relaxation massage, this can be that. If you want deeper therapeutic work, it can be that too.

Is this physical therapy?

No. This is not medical diagnosis or a physical therapy plan. It is therapeutic bodywork informed by manual therapy, movement practice, and years of hands-on experience.

What session length should I choose?

For most new clients, 75 minutes is the best starting point. 60 minutes works well for focused work on one area you know well. 90 minutes is for deeper or more complex work, or a fuller body session.

How often should I come?

One session can create real relief and clarity. Repeated sessions help reinforce a shift and give the body more time to develop a new pattern. Many clients come monthly; some more frequently while working through something specific.

Where are sessions held?

Sessions are held at 1300 Jackson St, Suite B200 in downtown Golden, Colorado. Any room-specific arrival details are provided when you book.

Do you work with injuries or surgery history?

Yes, when bodywork is appropriate. I have worked with many people navigating restriction, old injuries, scarring, and surgery history. This is not medical rehab or diagnosis; please mention relevant history when you book so we can plan accordingly.
